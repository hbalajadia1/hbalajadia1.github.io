Alan Mathison Turing

About His Life

The portrait of Alan Turing.

Alan Mathison Turing (June 1912 - June 1954) was a mathemetician who is famously attributed to decoding Nazi intelligence during the height of World War 2.

Turing was born in a Maide Vale, London, England. After expressing interest in math and science in primary school, he attended King's College in Cambridge. After this, he attended Princeton university. He was then employed by the Government Code and Cypher School, a British code-breaking organization.

One of his claims to fame comes from being able to decode codes used by the Germans during World War 2 and during Hitlers rise to power. He decoded the Enigma messages used by German intelligence, and helped create processes that could decode up to two messages per minute. This proved to be pivotal in changing the fate of the war.

Alan Turing commited suicide in June 1954 after being outed as a homosexual (since homosexuality was illegal in the UK).

Although his academic accomplishments were great, he is most known for his service in World War 2 and the controversy around his death. You can learn more by clicking here.

His Contributions to Society

Alan Turing invented the 'Turing Machine' which is the model of all stored-data devices.

His work on deciphering codes was said by Wiston Churchill to have shortened the war by two years.

He helped redesign the first commerically avaiable computer.
